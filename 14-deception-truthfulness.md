## Complete Honesty (1-20)

### 1. "I don't know."

**Genuine admission**
- ToBI: L* L* L+H* L-H%
- Stress: I don't **KNOW**
- Pause: I don't know?
- Simple truth, no pretense.

**Relieved confession**
- ToBI: L+H* L* L+H* L-L%
- Stress: I don't **know**
- Pause: I don't know.
- Exhaled, freed by admitting limits.

**Frustrated truth**
- ToBI: H* L* L+H* L-L%
- Stress: I **DON'T** know!
- Pause: I don't know!
- Annoyed but honest.

---

### 2. "Yes, I did it."

**Straightforward admission**
- ToBI: H* L* L+H* L* L-L%
- Stress: **YES** / I did it
- Pause: Yes / I did it.
- Clear, taking responsibility.

**Reluctant confession**
- ToBI: L* L* L+H* L* L-L%
- Stress: Yes / I did it
- Pause: Yes / I did it.
- Wish I didn't have to admit.

**Proud ownership**
- ToBI: H* H* L+H* L* L-H%
- Stress: **YES** / **I** did it!
- Pause: Yes! / I did it!
- Standing in action, unashamed.

- Grave, witnessing.

**Quiet certainty**

## Practice Notes

- **Deception detection** relies on vocal tells: pitch changes, hesitations, over-explanation, lack of detail
- **Honest speech** tends to be direct, detailed, contextual, emotionally congruent
- **White lies** serve social function but blur ethical lines
- **Evasion** avoids direct lying while concealing truth
- **Active lying** requires cognitive load (often betrayed by prosody)
- Cultural variations: some cultures value indirect communication (not deception)
- Professional contexts may require strategic ambiguity
- Self-deception: voice reflects our own belief (even if false)
- Ethical practice: Use these exercises to understand deception, not to harm

## Integration

Use these exercises to:
1. Recognize when you're being dishonest (even subtly)
2. Detect deception in others through vocal cues
3. Speak truth more directly and clearly
4. Navigate social situations requiring tact
5. Understand pressure to lie vs. commit to honesty
6. Develop congruence between inner truth and outer expression
7. Make conscious choices about transparency vs. privacy
