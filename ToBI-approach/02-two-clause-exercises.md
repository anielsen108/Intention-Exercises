# Two-Clause Exercises — Complex Intentions

100 exercises with compound sentences. Each clause carries its own intention, creating layered prosody. Practice shifting between two distinct emotional or communicative aims within a single utterance.

---

## 1. "I heard what you said, but I don't believe it."

**Skeptical-dismissive**
- ToBI: H* L* L* L* L-L% // H* L* H+L* L-L%
- Stress: I **HEARD** what you said // but I don't be**LIEVE** it
- Pause: I heard what you said // but I don't believe it.
- Acknowledging tone shifts to flat rejection.

**Curious-confused**
- ToBI: L+H* L* L* L* L-H% / L* L* L+H* L-H%
- Stress: I heard what you **SAID** / but I don't **believe** it
- Pause: I heard what you said / but I don't believe it?
- Open inquiry meets genuine puzzlement.

**Hurt-defensive**
- ToBI: L* H* L* L* L-L% / H* L* H+L* L-L%
- Stress: I **heard** what you said / but **I** don't believe it
- Pause: I heard what you said / but I don't believe it.
- Wounded reception shifts to protective denial.

## Practice Notes

- Two-clause sentences require breath management across the pivot point
- Notice where your pitch resets between clauses versus where it continues
- The conjunction (but, and, though, so, etc.) is your prosodic hinge — experiment with whether it belongs to the first or second clause rhythmically

- Advanced: Try mixing and matching intentions from different variations to create your own combinations
