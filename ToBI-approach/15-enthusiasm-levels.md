## Practice Notes

- **Enthusiasm** expressed through: pitch range (wider = more), tempo (faster = more), volume (louder = more), vowel quality (brighter = more)
- **Apathy** shows as: narrow pitch, slow tempo, soft volume, dark vowels
- **Appropriate calibration** crucial: too much enthusiasm seems fake; too little seems rude
- Physical embodiment: enthusiasm lives in the body (posture, gesture, facial expression)
- Sustaining enthusiasm requires genuine interest (fake enthusiasm exhausts)
- Cultural differences: some cultures reward restraint, others value expressiveness
- Professional contexts often require moderate enthusiasm (not extreme)
- Depression/exhaustion affect capacity for enthusiasm
- Practice accessing enthusiasm when feeling flat (exercise in activation)

## Integration

Use these exercises to:
1. Access genuine enthusiasm when needed
2. Modulate enthusiasm to appropriate levels
3. Recognize and avoid fake enthusiasm
4. Energize others through contagious passion
5. Respect others' enthusiasm levels
6. Maintain professional calibration
7. Reignite passion in work/relationships
