## Direct Confrontation (1-20)

### 1. "We need to talk."

**Serious/ominous**
- ToBI: H* L+H* L* L+H* L-L%
- Stress: **WE** need to **TALK**
- Pause: We need to talk.
- Heavy, relationship-threatening tone.

**Constructive invitation**
- ToBI: L+H* L+H* L* L+H* L-H%
- Stress: We **NEED** to talk

## Practice Notes

- **Conflict navigation** requires emotional regulation and strategic communication
- Escalation patterns: volume increases, pitch rises, tempo quickens, interruptions
- De-escalation: soften voice, slow pace, validate feelings, offer solutions
- "I" statements reduce defensiveness ("I feel X" vs. "You always Y")
- Repair attempts crucial: humor, affection, taking responsibility
- Some conflicts productive (clarifying values), some destructive (personal attacks)
- Practice staying physiologically calm (deep breathing) during conflict
- Cultural variations: direct vs. indirect conflict styles
- Healthy relationships navigate conflict; unhealthy ones avoid or weaponize it

## Integration

Use these exercises to:
1. Engage disagreement constructively
2. Set boundaries without aggression
3. De-escalate heated situations
4. Repair after conflict
5. Advocate for yourself effectively
6. Validate others while maintaining your stance
7. Distinguish productive from destructive conflict
