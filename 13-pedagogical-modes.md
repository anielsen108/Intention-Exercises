## Practice Notes

- **Pedagogical modes** vary by learner autonomy, teacher direction, and knowledge construction
- Direct instruction: clear, sequential, teacher-led (good for procedures, facts)
- Inquiry-based: question-driven, learner-discovery (good for concepts, critical thinking)
- Collaborative: peer learning, shared construction (good for complex problems)
- Tone matters: encouraging vs. discouraging profoundly affects learning
- Feedback should be specific, timely, and growth-oriented
- Balance praise (affirming effort) with challenge (raising standards)
- Different learners need different approaches (adapt fluidly)
- Metacognition (thinking about thinking) accelerates learning

## Integration

Use these exercises to:
1. Teach effectively in professional/personal contexts
2. Coach and mentor others
3. Explain complex ideas clearly
4. Foster critical thinking through questioning
5. Provide constructive feedback
6. Encourage persistence and growth mindset
7. Adapt teaching style to learner needs
