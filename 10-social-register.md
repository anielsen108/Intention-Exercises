## Practice Notes

- **Social register** varies by formality, vocabulary, syntax, and pronunciation
- Formal: complete sentences, Latinate words, precise diction
- Casual: contractions, colloquialisms, relaxed pronunciation
- Intimate: fragments, private language, soft voice
- Code-switching is a skill: matching register to context
- Mismatched register alienates (too formal) or offends (too casual)
- Professional success often requires facility across registers
- Some people have access to more registers than others (privilege marker)
- Practice doesn't mean abandoning your authentic voice

## Integration

Use these exercises to:
1. Navigate professional settings appropriately
2. Build rapport through register matching
3. Establish credibility in formal contexts
4. Connect authentically in casual contexts
5. Code-switch fluidly as context changes
6. Recognize and respect others' register choices
7. Expand your communicative range
