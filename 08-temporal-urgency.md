## Extreme Urgency (1-20)

**Immediate command**
- ToBI: H+L* L-L%

## Practice Notes

- **Temporal urgency** primarily expressed through tempo, volume, and pitch height
- Urgent = faster, louder, higher pitch, clipped consonants
- Patient = slower, softer, lower pitch, extended vowels
- Breathing patterns shift: rapid shallow (urgent) vs. deep slow (patient)
- Cultural variations: some cultures value "being on time," others "being in relationship"
- Mismatched urgency creates friction (one rushing, one meandering)
- Practice matching urgency to situation (genuine emergency vs. manufactured panic)
- Chronic urgency exhausts; practice calm pacing as well as speed

## Integration

Use these exercises to:
1. Calibrate urgency appropriately to situation
2. Manage deadline pressure vocally
3. Recognize when others are rushing you unfairly
4. Create urgency when necessary without panic
5. Practice patience and presence
6. Navigate cultural differences in pacing
7. Balance urgency with quality
